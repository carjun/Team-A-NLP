The world's biggest fast-food chain on Friday warned it's expecting April sales to slip and reported a quarterly profit that fell short of analysts' expectations. 	The company has been scrambling to revamp its menu, adding items like Fish McBites and McWraps, to lure diners. It has also been putting more emphasis on its value offerings to attract penny-pinchers. 	Its rivals, though, are nipping at its heels. Burger King recently cut the price of its Whopper Junior, and Wendy's has retooled its own value offerings. 	"The consumer overall is cutting back on their restaurant expenditures, so it's become a market share battle between companies," Lynne Collier, a senior analyst at Sterne, Agee & Leach, told the Daily News. 	While its profit margins slipped in the first quarter, it gained market share in the U.S. and other parts of the world. 	McDonald's executives said on a conference call that keeping prices low going forward would be critical to winning that fight. 	The company had sailed through the recession relatively unscathed, but began to stumble late last year. 	The April comments were particularly disappointing given the company only has measure up to a modest performance a year earlier, analysts said. The chain had struggled to match strong sales numbers from the first three months of 2012 when unusually warm weather boosted results.
