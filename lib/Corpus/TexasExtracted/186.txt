Wilson confirmed fatalities and 'probably over 100' injuriesAt least 160 injured and 5 to 15 dead, police sayExplosions rocked a fertilizer plant in West, Texas, on Wednesday evening as firefighters were battling an onsite blaze. The incident left at least 160 injured and 5 to 15 dead, police say.Waco police Sgt. William Patrick Swanton said early Thursday morning that the death toll is only an estimate as search and rescue operations remain under way in downtown West.Swanton says there is no indication the blast was anything other than an industrial accident, but the ATF will be investigating.Police say explosions at a fertilizer plant in West, Texas, could be heard up to 45 miles away.  Emergency crews were trying to evacuate people due to a fire at the plant when explosions rocked the small Texas city."The injuries that we are seeing are very serious,'' said Glenn Robinson, CEO of Hillcrest Baptist Medical Center. "There are a number of patients that will be going to surgery. ... It's a very, very unfortunate situation.''Texas Trooper D.L. Wilson  said he expected casualty numbers to grow as emergency workers go house to house in the area. Wilson confirmed at least 50 houses were damaged and a 50-unit apartment building was decimated. Another briefing was scheduled for 10 a.m. local time.Wilson said the damage was comparable to the destruction caused by the 1995 bomb blast that destroyed the Murrah Federal Building in Oklahoma City.
