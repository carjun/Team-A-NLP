Temperatures should fall to around 32 by 3 p.m. East winds should be between 10 and 15 mph, with gusts as high as 20 mph. The chance of precipitation was 80%. New snow accumulation of 2 to 4 inches was possible. The low should fall to around 26 under cloudy skies.Rain and possibly a thunderstorm were expected before noon in Houston, then a chance of rain. Some of the storms could produce heavy rain, and the steady temperature should be around 48 degrees. Winds could gust as high as 30 mph. The chance of precipitation was 100%. The low should fall to around 38 on Sunday night.A 40% chance of showers and thunderstorms was expected before noon in the Rio Grande Valley. Some of the storms could be severe, with hail, damaging winds and heavy rain in Brownsville. Skies should be mostly cloudy then gradually becoming sunny with a high near 76. Winds could gust as high as 32 mph. The low should fall to around 48 on Sunday night.Mostly cloudy skies were expected in the Texas Panhandle with a slight chance of snow and freezing drizzle. The low should fall to around 23 with wind chill values between 14 and 19 in Amarillo. The chance of precipitation was 20%.Copyright 2011 The Associated Press. All rights reserved.
