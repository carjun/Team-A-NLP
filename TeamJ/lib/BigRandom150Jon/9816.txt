(AP) - The Union Pacific Railroad plans to invest more than $400 million toward the cost to relocate its hub along the Mexican border from El Paso, Texas, to nearby Santa Teresa, N.M.Gov. Susana Martinez is expected to discuss the project Friday at a news conference in Santa Teresa.The Las Cruces Sun-News reports construction would begin after the governor and New Mexico Legislature pass a related tax incentive.The project could be worth hundreds of millions of dollars to the Santa Teresa area.There has been talk for years about relocating the hub. In 2006, the Omaha, Neb.-based company announced the planned relocation and in 2008 the company reported the project would create at least 60 new jobs and bring 285 jobs from El Paso.Information from: Las Cruces Sun-News, http://www.lcsun-news.comCopyright 2011 The Associated Press.
