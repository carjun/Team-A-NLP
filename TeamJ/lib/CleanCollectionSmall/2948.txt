He led the Cardinals to the franchise's first Super Bowl in 2008, where the team lost to the Pittsburgh Steelers.Before arriving in Arizona, Whisenhunt served as an assistant with the Pittsburgh Steelers for six years, the first three as tight ends coach and the last three as offensive coordinator. He had previously coached at the pro level with the New York Jets (tight ends, 2000), Cleveland Browns (special teams, 1999) and Baltimore Ravens (tight ends, 1997-98). He began his coaching career in the collegiate ranks, spending two seasons with Vanderbilt (1995-96).A native of Atlanta, Ga., and a graduate of Georgia Tech, Whisenhunt played nine NFL seasons with the Falcons (1985-88), Redskins (1989-90), and Jets (1991-93).
