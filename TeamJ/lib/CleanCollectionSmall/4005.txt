The medical examiner who oversaw the examination of the victims of the mass shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School said all were shot multiple times by rifle.   Connecticut Chief Medical Examiner Dr. H. Wayne Carver II told a news conference Saturday that the deaths are classified as homicides.   The victims had between 3 to 11 wounds each. He said he and his team worked through the night to examine and identify the victims, praising them for their work, including one examiner who, he believed, was on her first day on the job.   25 PhotosVigils for Conn. school shooting victimsHe said officials presented photographs to families to help them ID their children, who were all ages 6 or 7.    All of the victims' bodies have been released to the families or to funeral homes.
