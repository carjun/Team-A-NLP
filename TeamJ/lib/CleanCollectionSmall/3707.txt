The Minnesota Timberwolves officials announced on Saturday the return of point guard Ricky Rubio who has not played a basketball game in the National Basketball Association since March 9 when he tore his ACL and LCL on his left knee.  Rubio will make his first debut on Saturday night at the Target Center in Minneapolis when the Timberwolves host the Dallas Mavericks.  Rubio said in a statement "I'm excited to be back on the floor with my teammates and play in front of the greatest fans in the NBA at Target Center after a long recovery, this is the first step in me getting back on the court and helping my teammates. Thank you to the fans for their support over the last nine months."  Timberwolves coach Rick AdelmanRick Adelman did not say how minutes Rubio will play in his comeback game Saturday night.  Rubio will be sharin playing time at the point guard position with J J Barea and Luke Ridnor. Rubio will also be playing the shooting guard position since the Timeberwolves have injuries to Brandon RoyBrandon Roy, Chase BudingerChase Budinger and Malcolm Lee because Rubio is needed in that position as well.
