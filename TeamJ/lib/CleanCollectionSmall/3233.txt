Shinkle/POLITICOCloseBy BOBBY CERVANTES | 12/14/12 6:29 PM EST Dozens of demonstrators huddled outside the White House for a candlelight vigil Friday to demand President Barack Obama push for tighter guns laws, after the deadly school massacre in Newtown, Conn.Speakers from several progressive groups urged the president to include reforming the nations gun laws in his State of the Union address, making a broader appeal to address the issue in cities plagued with gun violence and other recent shootings in Aurora, Colo., and Wisconsin.Continue ReadingText Size-+resetThe country is with you. Lead us, one speaker said. The crowd chanted: Now is the time.The Associated Press reported 26 people, including 20 children, died in the shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School. The killer committed suicide at the school, and another person was found dead at a second scene, bringing the toll to 28.Jeff Blum, executive director of the progressive group USAction, said he expects the president to listen to the calls for reform.Its just depressing and outrage that the United State tolerates the level of gun violence we have, he said. The president is our leader, and we want him to lead.Obama became visibly emotional Friday afternoon while delivering a statement on the shootings, pledging to take meaningful action.I think its evident from his press conference that hes deeply moved, Blum said. Now he needs to exert direct leadership. We cant wait. There are too many. How many times does this have to happen?Stamford, Conn., native Emilie Pollack said she was about to take a final exam when she received a news alert on her phone Friday morning.Right now, its difficult to not be back in Connecticut, but its nice to see that people are reacting so strongly, the George Washington University student said. Ive always been pro-gun laws, and it shouldnt have happened.
