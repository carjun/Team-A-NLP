MalloyFollow Us OnFacebookFollow @washtimesQuestion of the DayWill Massachusetts Democrat John Kerry be considered for secretary of state?YesNoUndecidedOtherView resultsA gunman entered a rural Connecticut elementary school Friday morning  and killed more than two dozen people, including 18 children, according  to multiple reports.Horrifying details of the incident, one of the worst school shootings  in American history, came out throughout the morning at the Sandy Hook  Elementary School in Newtown, Conn., about 60 miles northeast of New  York City.According to reports from The Associated Press, the shooter was among  those dead. Witnesses interviewed on television reported hearing  hundreds of shots being fired as children and teachers cowered in  locked classrooms.The White House said President Obama was informed of the assault,  which took place as children were listening to morning announcements,  and Connecticut Gov. Dannel P. Malloy at the scene. Frightened children  could be seen being led from the schoolhouse to a nearby fire station  after the incident.Law enforcement officials were planning a briefing on the shooting around 1:30 p.m.In addition to the children, the schools principal was said to be one of the victims.Reuters, citing an NBC News report, reported that the shooter, an  adult, was dead and two handguns were recovered from the scene. There  were unconfirmed reports of a second shooter after witnesses reported  hearing dozens of shots, CBS reported.Sandy Hook Elementary  School teaches children from kindergarten through fourth grade  roughly  ages 5 to 10. All Newtown schools were placed in lockdown after the  shooting, the Newtown Public School District said.Newtown, with a population about 27,000, is in northern Fairfield  County, about 45 miles southwest of Hartford and 80 miles northeast of  New York City.Sandy Hook is one of four elementary schools in the district.
