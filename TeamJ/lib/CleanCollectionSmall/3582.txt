Reuters reported Sunday that survivors of Fridays massacre will attend classes in a neighbouring town when school reopens later this week. Police warned Sunday that misinformation was being widely circulated on social media sites and those spreading such information could be subject to arrest. Police said anything released outside official channels was most likely incorrect. Connecticut Medical Examiner Dr. H Wayne Carver II told reporters Saturday that most victims were first graders who suffered devastating injuries. Some victims were shot multiple times, Dr. Carver said. The primary weapon of choice was a long rifle. Dr. Carver was asked whether he became emotional while conducting the autopsies. For this one, not yet. Notice I said yet, he said. Authorities revealed Sunday that the shooter shot his mother repeatedly in the head just before killing 26 others at the school, the AP reported. His mother was known to store guns in her home and had just recently purchased the weapon her son used in the shooting. Connecticut State Police released a complete list of the victims of Fridays shooting which included the schools principal, Dawn Hochsprung, and school psychologist Mary Sherlach.
