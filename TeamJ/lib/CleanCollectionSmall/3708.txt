Authorities said on Saturday a man who was inside a hospital in Birmingham Alabama shot a Birmingham police officer and two hospital employees before the gunman was shot and killed by a second police officer.  Birmingham police officers were called to St. Vincent's Hospital around 4am Saturday to try to apprehend an armed man who was inside the hospital. The two police officers came in separate directions in the attempt to apprehended the gunman in the fifth floor of the hospital.  Birmingham police sergeant Johnny Williams said "When the officer encountered the suspect, there was immediate gunfire from the suspect." One police officer and two hospital employees were wounded.  A second police officer fired a shot that killed the gunman. The name of the suspect and the wounded persons names were not immediately released to the public.  Williams said detectives with the police force are trying to find out why the gunman entered the hospital.  St Vincent Hospital spokeswoman Liz Moore said at a press conference there were only a handful of patients that are being treated for heart problems and several nurses at the fifth floor at the time the gunman entered the fifth floor. Moore said the hospital is safe and secure and there was no interruption of any services during the incident.
