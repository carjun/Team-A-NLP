The report is based on a survey conducted from May 11-31, with 810 adults who are or had been unemployed at some point during the recession.The survey showed that most people who are out of work are optimistic about finding a decent replacement job. Of those idle workers, 58 percent are very or somewhat confident that the next job they find will be at least as good as the one they lost.
