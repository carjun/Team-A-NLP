Breaking News Facebook Says: "We're Not Shutting Down" Ends Dubious Rumor              (VIDEO) Filipino Devotees attended Black Nazarene procession in Manila    Manila :  Philippines|Jan 09, 2011By nicanorr        1 0     Views: 21      The Associated Press as of Saturday reported that Filipino devotees numbering about 1 million according to police, joined the 2011 annual procession of the Black Nazarene along the 5-kimeter route from the Rizal Park at the Luneta to Quiapo Church in Manila. Barefooted, the devotees with unique intentions of their own, forced their way into the sea of people who are all crowding the carriage to be with the Black Nazarene to have the chance to kiss and wipe him with towels to obtain his fabled curative powers. Joining the procession, especially that of rubbing elbows, climbing onto shoulders and being tossed just to get near Christ image they say is miraculous. Its a penitence . In the process, illness are cured, wishes, prayers and intentions are fulfilled, they say. But the scene is far from orderly Its scary for the uninitiated. Theres a possibility that youll be trampled upon by a sea of mad-like people. The Black Nazarene procession is no place for the weak-hearted and the physically disabled The scene of jostling, physically abled devotees trying to outdo one another for a better place happens throughout the 5 kilometer distance from Rizal Park up to Quiapo Church, as towels of all sorts flew here and there from the devotees to the Nazarene and back. It is no wonder why deaths and injuries results annually during such events as this. This particular event alone more than 450 followers got hurt, fainted or injured; at least 10 individuals were hospitalized. The scene as a whole was indeed touching. The Associated Press quoted Cardinal Gaudencio Rosales to have said: It was simply overwhelming seeing that sea of humanity. I asked, Jesus, can you see these people? Please answer their prayers The Philippines No. 1 pugilist Manny PacquiaoManny Pacquiao joined the throng of worshippers. A devout Roman Catholic, Pacquiao is reported to have visited the Nazarene several times, especially after his boxing bouts. About 3,500 police officers were assigned to secure the area, aside from doctors, nurses and the like to provide medical assistance.        1 of 38         Devotees struggle to hold the rope that pulls the carriage of the Black Nazarene during a religious procession in Manila      nicanorr is based in Catbalogan, Eastern Visayas, Philippines,and is Anchor for Allvoices Report Credibility           Share:                             Credibility      Reach             Global Reach This report's viewers come from the followingcountries:  United States of America (62.5%) Norway (12.5%) Australia (12.5%) Slovakia (12.5%)           READ MORE: Associated Press, video, Filipino Devotees, Black Nazarene, Procession, manila, 1 million, 2011 annual procession, 5-kimeter route, Rizal Park, quiapo church, barefooted, carriage, KISS, Wipe, towels, Miraculous, deaths, injuries, Cardinal Gaudencio Rosales, jesus, Manny Pacquiao, 3, 500 police officers, doctors, nurses, arts culture, Philippines, Roman Catholic Church in the Philippines, Cities in the Philippines, Church of the Nazarene, Pacquiao, boxers, Christian folklore, GaudencioRosales, nazarene process   More News From: Manila :  Philippines     Advertisement      Post a Comment Comments: 1            Reply  Posted By     nicanorr|about 4 hoursagoThe annual Black Nazarene procession in Manila is a much-awaited religious for RC devotees.
