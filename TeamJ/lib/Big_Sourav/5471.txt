Debbie Friedman passed away    Ashland :  KY :  USA|Jan 09, 2011By TracyJackson        1 0     Views: 115      Debbie FriedmanDebbie Friedman, reckoned as one of the most significant figures in contemporary Jewish music, who transformed the voice of American Jewish spirituality and prayers, passed away today at a hospital in Orange County, California. Debbie Friedman was hospitalized and kept on a respirator, after suffering from pneumonia and was in medically induced coma. She was over 50. Starting her three decades long career as a young woman during Vietnam War, Debbie Friedman took the accessibility and contemporary elements of the American folk music and blended it with Hebrew liturgical texts to create a powerful and euphoric body of work. During her 36-year long career, she successfully released over 20 albums and performed in hundreds of concerts around the world. Known for her famous song, "Mi Shebeirach", Debbie was also a legendary songwriter. She wrote many of her early songs and gathered numerous national and international prestigious awards.        1 of 2         Debbie Friedman 2      TracyJackson is based in Athens, Illinois, United States of America,and is Anchor for Allvoices Report Credibility           Share:                             Credibility      Reach             Global Reach This report's viewers come from the followingcountries:            READ MORE: Debbie Friedman passed away, passed away, obituaries-news, Ashland, Friedman, arts-culture-news, jewish music, Debbie, Entertainment Culture, Religion Belief, Debbie Friedman   More News From: Ashland :  KY :  USA     Advertisement      Post a Comment Comments: 0                  Enter your comment below       Add Your Comment  Save Changes  Cancel              Related Allvoices Contributions         Jewish folk singer Debbie Friedman dies By: rashidhussain      Debbie Friedman, who combined American folk music with Hebrew liturgical texts to create a popular contemporary form of Jewish music, died today at a hospital in Orange County, Calif.She had been hospitalized for pneumonia, according to a report in the Jerusalem Post.
