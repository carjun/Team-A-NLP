A spokesman for kelantan Flood Operations Room at Contingent Police Headquarters, said at 8 am this day four evacuation centers were opened bringing the total to 19 to accommodate the flood victims rose up to 1.571 people from 438 families compared to 1.008 people, on last night.  Five district affected with flood are Kota Baharu, Bachok, Pasir Mas, Tanah Merah and Pasir Puteh.  "The worst affected area was flooded Pasir Mas district with a total of 12 evacuation centers opened followed by Kota Baharu (three centers), while Tumpat, Tanah Merah, Pasir Puteh and Bachok each evacuation centers," he said.  A spokesman said, five routes in the Pasir Mas which is closed to light vehicles in KM3, Jalan Pengkalan Rakit, KM9 Jalan Pohon Tanjung-Rantau Panjang, Jalan Pohon Tanjung-Meranti, KM3 Jalan Tendong-Bunut Susu-Gelang Mas, KM10 Jalan Rantau Panjang-Panglima Bayu dan KM9 Jalan Cabang 4/Kampung Jambu/Meranti at Tumpat. The water level at Sungai Golok now at 10.28 meters above the danger level of 9 meters, while the other major rivers in Kelantan is at alert level.  At 3:51 pm (Malaysian time) - The floods reportedly worsened when the city is now cut off from Rantau Panjang flooded due to a meter deep.  Flood victims who moved to temporary evacuation centers now stands at 2.084 people all over Kelantan. All business premises in Rantau Panjang, a town bordering Thailand does not operate after the inundation of water since late last night when the water overflowed the banks of Sungai Golok.  Tonight, Director of the State Education Department, Hussain Awang visited several evacuation centers in Rantau Panjang where many schools were forced to close.  He advised parents not to let the kids play in order to avoid the flood water mishap occurred.  "School is closed cause they did not learn in the next few days. Empty space of time, including those in the evacuation centers should be used to review lessons on their own," he said.  Until this evening, there are still people who come to the region long evacuation centers to enroll because he feared the water level continues to increase at any time.  Meanwhile, in Pahang, the number of victims who are placed in relief centers in the state fell to 188 people from 47 families this morning over 244 people from 59 families, yesterday.  A spokesman for Operation Room Pahang police contingent headquarters, said all the victims of the Kuantan district was placed in four evacuation centers.  The area involved is the Park Blocks and Kampung Balok Any involved 104 people from 29 households were resettled in the House of People's Blocks, Kampung Bukit Palas, 33 people from seven families placed in Chenderawasih Hall, Village Mosquitoes, Beams, 33 people from seven families placed in Sekolah Agama Rakyat Pantai Balok, Chendor Middle Village, 18 people from four families located in the hall Chenor.  In Terengganu, a spokesman for the National Security Council (NSC) said the state, the overall flood victims who are still in evacuation centers at 9 am this morning dropped to 580 from 774 the night of Wednesday.  He said the flood situation in Hulu Terengganu recorded the highest number of victims of displacement are now almost fully recovered with only 87 people remain in evacuation centers than 367 people last night.  Meanwhile, Yayasan Pok and Kassim (YPK) began to send aid to flood victims in Kelantan, with two trucks full of cargo departing for the east coast states last night.  Its chairman, Datuk Seri Dr. Shahidan Kassim, said, relief food and other necessities will be distributed to flood victims through the cooperation of the leadership of the Barisan Nasional (BN) area.  Speaking to reporters after launching the relief assistance in Kangar, Perlis late last night, he said, YPK allocated about RM200, 000 for the aid mission.  He said he would also be in flooded areas to survey the distribution of assistance by volunteers YPK in the area.  Shahidan said, YPK, which is owned by his family foundation has no substantial provision and will only help the flood victims, just affordable.  Before this YPK help flood victims in Kedah and Perlis other victims of natural disasters in Indonesia.
