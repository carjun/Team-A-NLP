French Vogue has a new Editor-In-Chief    Paris :  France|Jan 07, 2011By lpetey        2 0     Views: 98      President of Conde Nast France Xavier Romatet held a press conference on Friday saying that Current Editor-In-Chief of French Vogue Carine Roitfeld will be replace by Emmanuelle Alt. She will be taking the reins beginning February 1st. Alt is quoted as saying "It's a great honor for me, but also a great pleasure to be at the head of Vogue Paris, which I know very well. Working with very talented teams, I will task myself with developing the incredible potential of Vogue Paris." Atl began her career at Elle in 1990 where at the young age of 20 she became fashion director. She had the same role at Mixte until 2000. Alt had worked under Roitfield as a fashion director before today's announcement that she will be taking over as Editor-In-Chief.        1 of 7         The Style of Emmanuelle Alt      lpetey is based in Arvada, Colorado, United States of America,and is Stringer for Allvoices Report Credibility           Share:                             Credibility      Reach             Global Reach This report's viewers come from the followingcountries:  United States of America (48.5%) France (21.6%) Germany (9.3%) Senegal (9.3%) 7 other countries (8.2%)           READ MORE: France, Paris, mass media, Carine Roitfeld, Emmanuelle Alt, publishing, Vogue, lifestyle-news, Elle, editor, fashion, magazine, Couture, arts-culture-news, editor-in-chief   More News From: Paris :  France     Advertisement      Post a Comment Comments: 1            Reply  Posted By     Adebanjos|1 dayagoa very good one, thanks for sharing. rated!
