A social media firestorm was ignited on Saturday by an article published on the Weekly World News, a news-parody website claiming to be the source of The Worlds Only Reliable News.".  In this article It was falsely reported that Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg had announced at a press conference outside his office in Palo Alto, CA "Facebook has gotten out of control and the stress of managing this company has ruined my life. I need to put an end to all the madness." The article goes on to say that the site will be completely taken down with no access to pictures, posts, videos, and such and suggests that you back all that data up at different location.  GMANews.TV went so far as to research this hoax on Snopes.com  the definitive Internet reference source for urban legends, folklore, myths, rumors, and misinformation"  common Internet hoaxes like this one include rumors that a free web-based service would soon begin mass deletion of accounts" or start charging for access." to further dispel the insipid rumors.  What we do know is true is that Facebook recently signed a deal netting $500 million from Goldman Sachs. The likelihood of Facebook shutting down after inking a deal of this magnitude...well you decide, oh and Happy April Fools day early!
