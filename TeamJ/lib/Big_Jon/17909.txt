Timeline of the Mexican Drug War - Wikipedia, the free encyclopediaTimeline of the Mexican Drug WarFrom Wikipedia, the free encyclopediaJump to: navigation,searchThe timeline of the most relevant events in the Mexican Drug War is set out below. Although violence between drug cartels had been occurring for three decades, the Mexican government held a generally passive stance regarding cartel violence through the 1980s and early 2000s. That changed on December 11, 2006, when the newly elected President Felipe Caldern sent 6,500 Mexican Army soldiers to the state of Michoacn to end drug violence there. This action is regarded as the first major retaliation made against the cartel violence, and is generally viewed as the starting point of the Mexican Drug War between the government and the drug cartels.[1] As time progressed, Caldern continued to escalate his anti-drug campaign, in which there are now about 45,000 troops involved along with state and federal police forces.[2]Contents1 20062 20073 20084 20095 20106 20117 References8 External links[edit] 2006November 25 - Popular singer Valentn Elizalde is gunned down along with his manager (and best friend) Mario Mendoza Grajeda, and driver Reynaldo Ballesteros.
